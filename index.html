Relax Tips for Kids

Follow these fun exercises to stay calm before or during your shot! ğŸ’–

Take Deep Breaths

Inhale slowly through your nose, hold for 3 seconds, then exhale through your mouth. Repeat 3 times to feel calm.

Hug Your Favorite Toy

Hold your favorite toy close. Hugging something soft can make you feel safe and relaxed.

Imagine a Happy Place

Close your eyes and think about a place you love. Imagine the colors, sounds, and smells to feel relaxed.

Blow Bubbles

Blowing bubbles or pretending to blow them slowly helps your body relax and distracts from the needle.

Gentle Stretching

Move your arms and legs slowly or shake your hands to release tension before the injection.

Parent Tips

Help your child feel safe and calm during injections with these tips:

Distraction Works

Bring a toy, play a game, or watch a short video to distract your child during shots. Distraction reduces anxiety and makes the procedure faster.

Talk Calmly

Explain what will happen in a gentle, reassuring voice. Avoid words like "pain" or "hurt"; instead, focus on bravery and fun rewards.

Positive Reinforcement

Praise bravery and offer rewards like stickers or extra playtime. Positive reinforcement encourages courage in future visits.

Practice Breathing

Teach your child simple deep-breathing exercises or blowing bubbles. Slow breathing helps reduce anxiety and keeps them calm.

Comfort Items

Allow your child to hold a favorite toy, blanket, or stuffed animal. Familiar items provide comfort and security.

Stay Calm Yourself

Children pick up on your emotions. Stay relaxed and positive to help them remain calm and confident.

Prepare in Advance

Talk about the visit beforehand and explain what will happen in simple terms. Knowing what to expect reduces fear.

Reward After

Give a small treat, praise, or extra playtime after the shot. This teaches your child that bravery is recognized and celebrated.
